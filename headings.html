THE ROAD NOT TAKEN

ANSWER THE FOLLOWING QUESTIONS BRIEFLY:

1. Where were the two roads? What was the poet’s difficulty?

When the poet was travelling he came across two diverging roads in a forest. It was difficult for him to decide which road he should take. Both roads seemed equally good and similar.

2. How did the poet make his choice about the road?

The poet stood for a long time and looked at the diverging roads. Then he took the one which he considered better than the other one. It seemed to him grassy and less worn out. He thought that he would come back one day to try the other road.

3. Did the poet make his choice without thinking?

No, the poet infact took a long time to make his choice. He looked down the road as far as he could . Then he selected that one which seemed to have a better claim. It was grassy and wanted wear.

4. Was the poet sure of coming back to the first road some day?

. No, the poet was not sure if he would return to take up the first road someday. The poet knew that one road would lead to another and it might not be possible to come back and try the first one.
